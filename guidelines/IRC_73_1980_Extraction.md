# IRC:73-1980 — Geometric Design Standards for Rural (Non-Urban) Highways
## Structured Extraction for DPR Analyzer Pro

---

## 1. SCOPE & APPLICABILITY

- Applies to **non-urban (rural) highways** in open country outside built-up areas
- **Not applicable** to: urban roads, city streets, expressways, or road intersections
- Standards are **advisory** but should be aimed at or above minimum
- Geometric deficiencies are costly/impossible to rectify later — must be addressed at DPR stage

---

## 2. CLASSIFICATION OF NON-URBAN ROADS

| Code | Road Class | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| NH | National Highways | Connect major ports, state capitals, foreign highways |

| ODR | Other District Roads | Serve rural production areas to market centres/taluka HQ |

### 5.5 Carriageway Width

**Table 7 — Width of Carriageway**

|------------|-------------------------------|----------------------------|----------------------------|
| 3.75 m** | 7.0 m | 7.5 m | 3.5 m |

**On VR may be restricted to 3.0 m normally.

> Transition from single to two lanes or two to four lanes: taper of 1 in 15 to 1 in 20.

### 5.6 Shoulder Width
- Shoulder width = ½ × (Roadway width − Carriageway width) [derived from Tables 5, 6, 7]
- General shoulder width for multi-lane: **2.5 m**

### 5.7 Median Width
- Minimum desirable: **5 m** (rural highways)
- Minimum where land restricted: **3 m**
- Long bridges/viaducts: minimum **1.5 m**, never < **1.2 m**
- Transition where unavoidable: 1 in 15 to 1 in 20

### 5.8 Roadway Width at Cross-Drainage Structures

**Culverts (≤ 6 m span) — Plain & Rolling:** Roadway width = Table 5 values (outside to outside of parapet walls)

**Culverts (≤ 6 m span) — Mountainous & Steep:** Clear roadway (inside to inside parapet/kerbs):
- All roads except VR: as per Table 6
- VR minimum: as per Table 6; desirable = **4.25 m**

**Bridges (> 6 m span) — Clear width between kerbs:**
- Single-lane bridge: **4.25 m**
- Two-lane bridge: **7.5 m**
- Multi-lane bridge: **3.5 m per lane + 0.5 m per carriageway**
- Causeways/submersible bridges: minimum **7.5 m** (between kerbs)
- Footpath (where provided): ≥ **1.5 m**

### 5.9 Pavement Camber/Crossfall

**Table 8 — Camber/Crossfall Values**

| Surface Type | Camber/Crossfall |
|-------------|-----------------|
| High type bituminous / cement concrete | 1.7–2.0% (1:60 to 1:50) |
| Thin bituminous surfacing | 2.0–2.5% (1:50 to 1:40) |
| Water bound macadam / gravel | 2.5–3.0% (1:40 to 1:33) |
| Earth | 3.0–4.0% (1:33 to 1:25) |

- Earth shoulders: crossfall ≥ pavement crossfall + **0.5%**, minimum **3%**
- Paved shoulders: appropriate crossfall per Table 8
- Superelevated sections: shoulders same crossfall as pavement

## 8. HORIZONTAL ALIGNMENT

### 8.1 General Rules
- Horizontal alignment must be fluent, blending with surrounding topography
- Long tangents > **3 km** to be avoided
- Sharp curves at end of long tangents: **prohibited**
- Curve length minimum **150 m** for 5° deflection; +30 m per 1° decrease in deflection angle
- Broken-back curves to be avoided; minimum tangent between reverse curves = **10 seconds travel time**
- Compound curves: ratio of flatter to sharper radius ≤ **1.5:1**

### 8.2 Superelevation

**Formula:** e = V²/225R

Where: e = superelevation (m/m); V = speed (km/h); R = radius (m)

**Maximum Superelevation Limits:**
| Terrain | Maximum e |
|---------|----------|
| Plain & Rolling | 7% |
| Snow-bound areas | 7% |
| Hilly (not snow-bound) | 10% |

|--------------------|-----------|-----------|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| 20 | 50 | 60 | 70 | 90 | 100 |
| 25 | 70 | 90 | 110 | 140 | 150 |
| 30 | 100 | 130 | 160 | 200 | 240 |
| 35 | 140 | 180 | 220 | 270 | 320 |
| 40 | 180 | 240 | 280 | 350 | 420 |
| 50 | 280 | 370 | 450 | 550 | 650 |
| 65 | 470 | 620 | 750 | 950 | 1100 |
| 80 | 700 | 950 | 1100 | 1400 | 1700 |
| 100 | 1100 | 1500 | 1800 | 2200 | 2600 |

**Methods of Attaining Superelevation (3 methods):**
1. Revolving pavement about centre line
2. Revolving pavement about inner edge
3. Revolving pavement about outer edge

**Rate of Change:** Longitudinal slope of pavement edge vs centre line ≤ 1 in 150 (plain/rolling); ≤ 1 in 60 (mountainous/steep)

### 8.4 Transition Curves
- Spiral (clothoid) transition curves mandatory at both ends of circular curve
- **Minimum Transition Length — Two Criteria (larger value adopted):**

**Criterion 1 (Rate of Change of Centrifugal Acceleration):**

L_s = 0.0215V³/CR

Where: C = 80/(75+V) subject to max 0.8 and min 0.5; R = radius (m)

**Criterion 2 (Rate of Change of Superelevation):**
- Plain & Rolling: L_s = 2.7V²/R
- Mountainous & Steep: L_s = 1.0V²/R

### 8.6 Set-back Distance at Horizontal Curves

**Formula:** m = R − (R−n) cos θ; where θ = S/2(R−n) radians

Where: m = minimum set-back (m from road centre); R = radius at centre line (m); n = distance between road centre line and centre line of inside lane (m); S = sight distance (m)

## 9. VERTICAL ALIGNMENT

### 9.1 General Rules
- No grade change within **150 m** desirably
- Broken-back grade lines (two vertical curves same direction, short tangent between) to be avoided
- Culvert/minor bridge decks to follow flanking road profile without break

### 9.2 Gradients

**Table 19 — Gradients for Roads in Different Terrains**

| Terrain | Ruling Gradient | Limiting Gradient | Exceptional Gradient |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Plain or Rolling | 3.3% (1:30) | 5% (1:20) | 6.7% (1:15) |
| Mountainous (altitude ≤ 3000 m MSL) | 5% (1:20) | 6% (1:16.7) | 7% (1:14.3) |
| Steep terrain (≤ 3000 m MSL) | 6% (1:16.7) | 7% (1:14.3) | 8% (1:12.5) |

> **Mandatory Limits:**
> - Exceptional gradient: max length **100 m at a stretch**; mountainous & steep — successive exceptional stretches separated by minimum **100 m** at limiting gradient or flatter
> - Rise in elevation over **2 km** ≤ 100 m (mountainous) or ≤ 120 m (steep)
> - Isolated over-bridges/slow-traffic roads: flatter gradient of **2%** desirable
> - Minimum gradient for drainage (unkerbed): **0.5%** (lined side drains), **1.0%** (unlined)

**Grade Compensation at Horizontal Curves:**

Grade compensation (%) = (30+R)/R, maximum 75/R

(Not required for gradients flatter than 4%; corrected gradients need not be eased below 4%)

### 9.3 Vertical Curves

**Minimum length of vertical curves:** Both summit and valley curves designed as square parabolas.

**Table 20 — Minimum Length of Vertical Curves**

| Design Speed (km/h) | Max Grade Change Not Requiring Vertical Curve (%) | Minimum Length of Vertical Curve (m) |
|--------------------|-------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| ≤ 35 | 1.5 | 15 |
| 40 | 1.2 | 20 |
| 50 | 1.0 | 30 |
| 65 | 0.8 | 40 |
| 80 | 0.6 | 50 |
| 100 | 0.5 | 60 |

### 9.4 Summit Curves — Length Formulae

**For Safe Stopping Sight Distance:**

Case L > S: **L = NS²/4.4**

Case L < S: **L = 2S − 4.4/N**

**For Intermediate or Overtaking Sight Distance:**

Case L > S: **L = NS²/9.6**

Case L < S: **L = 2S − 9.6/N**

Where: N = deviation angle (algebraic difference of grades); L = length of vertical curve (m); S = sight distance (m)

### 9.5 Valley Curves — Length Formulae

**For Headlight Sight Distance:**

Case L > S: **L = NS²/(1.50 + 0.035S)**

Case L < S: **L = 2S − (1.50 + 0.035S)/N**

## 10. CO-ORDINATION OF HORIZONTAL & VERTICAL ALIGNMENTS

**Mandatory Requirements:**
- Horizontal and vertical curves to coincide as far as possible; lengths to be roughly equal
- Horizontal curve should be somewhat **longer** than vertical curve
- Sharp horizontal curves at or near apex of pronounced summit/sag vertical curves: **prohibited**
- Degree of curvature must balance with gradients
- Broken-back curves (plan & profile) to be avoided

---

## 11. LATERAL & VERTICAL CLEARANCES AT UNDERPASSES

### 11.1 Lateral Clearance
- Minimum lateral clearance (extreme edge of carriageway to face of nearest support) = **normal shoulder width**
- Lower category hill roads with narrow shoulders: increase roadway width per para 6.3

### 11.2 Vertical Clearance
- Minimum vertical clearance at underpasses: **5.0 m** (after allowance for future raising/strengthening)

## 12. DPR COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST — IRC:73-1980

### Section A: General & Classification
- [ ] Road classification correctly identified (NH/SH/MDR/ODR/VR)
- [ ] Terrain correctly classified per Table 1 (cross slope basis, not short isolated stretches)
- [ ] Design speed (ruling + minimum) correctly selected per Table 2 for road class and terrain

### Section B: Cross-Section
- [ ] Land width (ROW) as per Table 3 — correct road class and terrain
- [ ] Building line and control line standards as per Table 4
- [ ] Roadway width as per Table 5 (plain/rolling) or Table 6 (mountainous/steep)
- [ ] Carriageway width as per Table 7
- [ ] Shoulder width derived correctly (Table 5/6 minus Table 7, divided by 2)
- [ ] Median width ≥ 5 m (rural); ≥ 3 m where restricted; ≥ 1.2 m at bridges
- [ ] Culvert widths (≤ 6 m span) as per Tables 5/6 (plain/rolling) or Table 6 inside clear (hilly)
- [ ] Bridge widths: single ≥ 4.25 m; two-lane ≥ 7.5 m; multi-lane ≥ 3.5 m per lane + 0.5 m
- [ ] Footpath ≥ 1.5 m where provided
- [ ] Passing places provided on single-lane mountain/steep roads (3.75 m × 30 m/20 m; 2–3 per km)
- [ ] Camber/crossfall as per Table 8 for surface type
- [ ] Earth shoulder crossfall ≥ pavement crossfall + 0.5%, minimum 3%
- [ ] Snow-clearance roads: roadway width increased by 1.5 m (MDR, ODR, VR)

### Section C: Design Traffic & Capacity
- [ ] Traffic converted to PCU using Table 9 equivalency factors
- [ ] Carriageway width justified against design traffic vs Table 10 capacity values

### Section D: Sight Distance
- [ ] SSD provided as per Table 11 (based on ruling design speed)
- [ ] OSD provided on maximum feasible length of road (Table 12)
- [ ] ISD adopted where OSD not feasible (Table 13)
- [ ] Minimum visibility never less than SSD on any section
- [ ] Measurement criteria as per Table 14 (eye 1.2 m; object 0.15 m for SSD; 1.2 m for ISD/OSD)
- [ ] Valley curve headlight sight distance = SSD (Table 11); headlight ht 0.75 m, beam 1° upward
- [ ] Set-back clearance at horizontal curves calculated and obstructions cleared

### Section E: Horizontal Alignment
- [ ] Long tangents > 3 km flagged and justified
- [ ] Sharp curves not introduced at end of long tangents
- [ ] Curve length ≥ 150 m for 5° deflection; +30 m per 1° decrease
- [ ] Broken-back curves avoided; minimum tangent = 10 seconds travel time between reverse curves
- [ ] Compound curve ratio ≤ 1.5:1
- [ ] Superelevation calculated per formula (e = V²/225R); within max limits (Table 15)
- [ ] Radii not less than ruling minimum (Table 16); absolute minimum only in exceptional cases
- [ ] Transition curves provided; length as per larger of two criteria (Table 17)
- [ ] Rate of superelevation change ≤ 1:150 (plain/rolling); ≤ 1:60 (mountainous/steep)
- [ ] Extra carriageway width at curves as per Table 18
- [ ] Hair-pin bend design parameters met (all criteria in Section 8.7 above)
- [ ] Minimum 60 m separation between successive hair-pin bends

### Section F: Vertical Alignment
- [ ] Ruling gradient within limits per Table 19 for terrain class
- [ ] Limiting gradient sections flagged; exceptional gradient sections max 100 m, separated by 100 m flatter
- [ ] Rise in elevation over 2 km ≤ 100 m (mountainous) / 120 m (steep)
- [ ] Minimum drainage gradient: 0.5% (lined), 1.0% (unlined)
- [ ] Grade compensation applied at horizontal curves where gradient > 4%
- [ ] Minimum vertical curve length as per Table 20
- [ ] Summit curve length calculated using correct formula (SSD/ISD/OSD as applicable)
- [ ] Valley curve length calculated using headlight sight distance formula
- [ ] No grade change within 150 m desirably
- [ ] Broken-back grade lines avoided

### Section G: Co-ordination
- [ ] Horizontal and vertical curve vertices coincide
- [ ] Horizontal curve length ≥ vertical curve length
- [ ] No sharp horizontal curves at summit/sag curve apex
- [ ] 3D design checked — plan and profile not designed independently

### Section H: Underpasses
- [ ] Lateral clearance = normal shoulder width (minimum)
- [ ] Vertical clearance ≥ 5.0 m
