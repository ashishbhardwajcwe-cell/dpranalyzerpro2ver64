# IRC:SP:42-2014 — Guidelines of Road Drainage (First Revision)
## Structured Extraction for DPR Analyzer Pro
> **Scope:** Non-urban rural roads. Excludes urban roads (IRC:SP:50), hill roads (IRC:SP:48), airfield pavements, and bridge/culvert structural design (IRC:5, IRC:78).
> **Format:** Mandatory requirements, design tables, formulas, and DPR compliance checklist. Theory, derivations, and worked examples excluded.

---

## PART 1: GENERAL CRITERIA & MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS

### 1.1 Two Mandatory Drainage Criteria (Cl. 3)
A road drainage system **must** satisfy both:
1. **Functional criterion** — Drain surface and subsurface water away from the roadway, maintaining safe and comfortable driving conditions at all times.
2. **Structural criterion** — Prevent premature structural failure caused by water ingress into pavement layers.

### 1.2 Minimum Formation Level Requirements (Cl. 3)
- Bottom of subgrade **shall be 0.5 m to 1.00 m above HFL** (High Flood Level), depending on soil type (refer Fig. 3.2 of standard).
- HFL shall be determined by inspection, local enquiry, and flood records.

### 1.3 Mandatory Site Visit (Cl. 7.1.1)
A site visit by the drainage engineer **is obligatory** before hydraulic design. Required data to collect:
- Natural drainage system and tributary rivers/nallas
- Topographic features within RoW
- Existing drains, CD works, water bodies
- Ponding/waterlogged/flooded areas
- Rainfall records, HFL, soil/subsoil, high-water marks

## PART 2: SURFACE DRAINAGE DESIGN REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 Minimum Longitudinal Gradient (Cl. 4.4.1)
**Minimum longitudinal gradient = 0.3%** for all road types.
*(Essential to prevent standing water on road surface; applies to kerbed sections in particular.)*

### 2.2 Pavement Cross-Fall / Camber (Cl. 4.4.2)

**Table 4.1 — Mandatory Camber/Cross-Fall Values**

| Surface Type | Non-Kerbed Roads | Roads with Kerb |
|---|---|---|
| Earthen, Gravelled, or WBM Surface | 3–5% | — |
| Thin open-graded bituminous surfacing | 2.5–3% | 2.5–3% |
| High-type bituminous surfacing | 2.0–2.5% | 2.5% |
| Cement concrete (transverse tine/brush texture) | 2.0–2.5% | 2.5% |
| Cement concrete (longitudinal tine texture) | 2.5% | 2.5% |

**Additional rule:** Earthen/granular shoulders shall be provided with **0.5% more cross-slope than the paved surface** (Fig. 4.2 of standard).

### 2.3 Drainage of High Embankments (Cl. 4.4.9)
- Flumes/chutes shall be provided at regular intervals (generally **20 m c/c** as per IRC:SP:50).
- Energy dissipater required at toe for plain chutes (stepped chutes are self-dissipating; no additional dissipater required).
- Stone pitching **1.0 m wide on both sides** of chutes; filter bed under pitching.
- Erosion control mats (jute/coir) as per MORTH Section 700, Clause 706.

### 2.4 Drainage of Reinforced Soil Structures (Cl. 4.4.10)
- A longitudinal porous/open-jointed pipe of **not less than 150 mm diameter** shall be used if foundation soil is not sufficiently pervious.
- Sealed kerb channel at back of paved shoulder/edge of carriageway required at top of wall.
- Surface runoff must be diverted at top of slope to prevent flow over face.

### 2.5 Bridge Deck Drainage (Cl. 9.6)
- Minimum cross-slope on bridge deck: **1.0%**
- Minimum longitudinal grade on bridge deck: **0.5%**
- Gutter grading: **1.0%**
- Inlet opening shall be flared to **at least twice the size of the drain pipe** (grating reduces effective area by ~50%).
- Kerb outlet pipe (for kerbed pavements): **150 to 200 mm dia PVC or HDPE**
- Drain spout spacing: **3 m on both edges** of bridge (standard); increase spout numbers for wider bridges.

### 3.4 Capillary Cut-Off Requirements (Cl. 5.3, 5.3.1)
- Required when water table or HFL is high such that **bottom of subgrade has < 0.6–1.0 m clearance above design HFL** (as per IRC:37).

**Capillary Cut-Off Sand Blanket Thickness Formula (from IRC:34):**
> `t = (2 × d₁ × d₂) / (d₁ + d₂)`
Where:
- `t` = thickness of sand layer (cm)
- `d` = mean particle diameter (mm); `d = (2 × d₁ × d₂) / (d₁ + d₂)`
- `d₁` = aperture size of sieve through which fraction passes (mm)
- `d₂` = aperture size of sieve on which fraction is retained (mm)

**Indicative value:** Sand passing 5 mm sieve and retained on 0.3 mm → blanket thickness ≈ **12 cm** (use 15 cm).
- A cushion of **10–15 cm sand/granular material** over the cut-off layer is required.

## PART 5: DESIGN FORMULAE

### 5.1 Rational Method — Peak Runoff Formula (Cl. 6.4.2)
**Applicable for catchments up to 25 sq. km**
> **Q = 0.028 × P × f × A × I**
- `Q` = maximum runoff (cumecs)
- `A` = catchment area (hectares)
- `I` = design rainfall intensity (cm/hr) for selected return period and duration = time of concentration
- `P` = runoff coefficient (Table 6.5)
- `f` = areal spread factor (convert point rainfall to areal mean; from Fig. 6.1 of standard based on catchment area in hectares)

### 5.2 Dickens Formula — Flood Peak Estimation (Cl. 6.4.1)
> **Q = C × A^(3/4)**
- `Q` = maximum flood peak (cumecs)
- `A` = catchment area (km²)
- `C` = Dickens constant (region-specific)

| Region | C Value |
|---|---|
| North Indian Plains | 6 |
| North Indian Hilly Regions | 11–14 |
| Central India | 14–28 |
| Coastal Andhra and Orissa | 22–28 |

### 5.3 SCS Curve Number Method — Direct Runoff (Cl. 6.4.3)
> **Q = (P – 0.2S)² / (P + 0.8S)**
- `Q` = storm runoff (mm)
- `P` = storm rainfall (mm)
- `S` = potential maximum retention (mm) = **(25400/CN) – 254**
- `CN` = Curve Number (from Annexures III(a)/(b) based on soil group, land use, AMC)
- Initial abstraction `Iₐ = 0.2S`

**Table 6.8 — Curve Numbers (CN) for AMC-I and AMC-III corresponding to AMC-II**
*(Select values)*

**SCS Peak Discharge Formula:**
> **Qₚ = qᵤ × A × Q × Fₚ**
- `Qₚ` = peak discharge (cumecs)
- `qᵤ` = unit peak discharge (cumec/km²/mm)
- `A` = catchment area (km²)
- `Q` = runoff volume (mm)
- `Fₚ` = pond and swamp adjustment factor

**Fₚ Values:**

| % Pond and Swamp Area | Fₚ |
|---|---|
| 0 | 1.00 |
| 0.2 | 0.97 |
| 1 | 0.87 |
| 3 | 0.75 |
| 5 | 0.72 |

**Unit Peak Discharge Formula (FHWA):**
> **qᵤ = 0.000431 × 10^(C₀ + C₁ log(Tc) + C₂(log(Tc))²)**
- `Tc` = time of concentration (hours)
- `C₀, C₁, C₂` = constants from Annexure III(c) based on Iₐ/P ratio and rainfall distribution type

### 5.4 Time of Concentration Formulae (Cl. 6.3.5)

**IRC:SP:13 Formula:**
> **Tc = 0.84 × (L³/H)^0.385** (hours)
- `L` = distance from most remote point to outlet (km)
- `H` = fall in level from most remote point to outlet (m)

**Kirpich Equation:**
> **T (min) = f(L, S)** → **T = function of L^0.77 / S**
- `L` = distance from most remote point to outlet (m)
- `S` = slope of catchment

**Total Tc:**
> **Tc = Tt1 + Tt2 + Tt3**
*(sum of shallow concentrated flow, sheet flow, and channel flow travel times)*

**Shallow Concentrated Flow:**
> `tt = L / (3600 × V)` ; `V = k × S^0.5` (k from Table 6.6)

**Sheet Flow:**
> `t₂ = 0.092 × (n × L)^0.8 / (P₂^0.5 × S^0.4)` where `P₂` = 2-year 24-hour rainfall (mm)

**Channel Flow (Manning's):**
> `V = (1/n) × R^(2/3) × S^(1/2)` ; `Tt = L / (3600 × V)`

## PART 6: HYDRAULIC DESIGN OF ROAD DRAINAGE

### 6.1 Manning's Equation (Cl. 7.2.1)

> **Q = (1/n) × A × R^(2/3) × S^(1/2)** — discharge
> **V = (1/n) × R^(2/3) × S^(1/2)** — mean velocity
> **Q = K × S^(1/2)** — channel conveyance; K = (1/n)(A × R^(2/3))

Where:
- `Q` = discharge (m³/s)
- `V` = mean velocity (m/s)
- `n` = Manning's roughness coefficient
- `R` = hydraulic radius (m) = Area / Wetted Perimeter
- `S` = energy slope (bed slope of drain)
- `A` = cross-sectional area of flow (m²)

**Critical Depth Check (rectangular channel):**
> `dc = (Q²/g)^(1/3) / b`
If `dn < dc` → slope is steep / supercritical; redesign or provide stepped chutes.

### 6.2 Triangular Gutter Flow Formulae (Cl. 7.2.2)

**For right triangular section (urban gutters):**
> **Q = (0.315/n) × Z × d^(8/3) × S^(1/2)** *(in terms of depth)*
> **Q = (0.317/n) × S^(1/2) × T^(8/3) / Z^(5/3)** *(in terms of spread width T)*

**For V-shaped section:**
> **Q = (1/n) × Fₙ(Z) × d^(8/3) × S^(1/2)**
> `Fₙ(Z) = 0.63 Z^(4/3) / (1 + Z₁/Z₂)^(5/3)` (for two-sided V)
> Depth: `d = 1.1892 × (n × Q)^(3/8) / (S^(3/16) × Z^(5/8))` (approximately)

**Spread width equation for both types:**
> `Q = (0.317/n) × Sx^(1/2) × T^(8/3)` where `Sx = S₁·S₂/(S₁+S₂)` for V-shaped

**Permissible spread:**
- National & State Highways: **Shoulder + 1 m**
- District Roads: **Up to shoulder**
- Village Roads: **½ driving lane**

### 6.4 Minimum Drain Channel Grade (Cl. 7.2.4, Step-3)
- **Minimum grade: 0.3%** for all roadside drains.
- Grade need not equal highway grade but shall follow existing ground profile to avoid excessive cutting.
- Stepped chutes/drop structures required where ground slope is steep (supercritical flow).
- **Minimum freeboard: 0.1 m to 0.15 m** over design water level.

### 6.5 Drain Location — Minimum Setback from Embankment (Cl. 7.1.5)
- Unlined drain: **minimum distance = 2H from toe of embankment** (H = height of road top above ground).
- If drain is within 4H:1V line → drain **must be lined** with stone/brick masonry or concrete.

## PART 7: ROADSIDE DITCHES & DRAINS — DESIGN SPECIFICATIONS

### 7.1 Drain Cross-Section Types (Cl. 8.2)
Acceptable cross-sections: Triangular, Rectangular, Parabolic, U-shaped, Trapezoidal, V-shaped.
- **Minimum bottom width for ease of cleaning: 0.3 m**
- **Invert level: water surface at design discharge shall be at least 0.3–0.6 m below drainage layer of pavement**
- Earthen drain: must not be allowed to run full for extended duration (causes side erosion).
- RCC lining joints: sealed at **8–10 m intervals** or as specified in contract.

### 7.2 Drain Lining Specifications (Cl. 8.4)

| Lining Type | Specification |
|---|---|
| RCC lining | Suitable for any cross-section; M-20 grade concrete; joints at 8–10 m |
| Random rubble masonry | M-20 grade concrete + M-15 bed concrete |
| Brick lining | 1:4 cement-sand mortar; M-15 grade bed concrete; sand bed |
| Stone slab lining | M-15 grade concrete + gravel/sand bed |

### 7.3 Hilly Terrain Side Drains (Cl. 8.4.5, 8.4.6, 8.5)
- Width restricted to **60 cm** (wider cuts cause instability of hillside).
- Triangular drain (hilly): 100 mm thick stone pitching, 20–25 mm cement mortar (1:4), 50 mm bed concrete, sand bed.
- Trapezoidal drain (hilly): RR masonry (1:4) or precast M-25 concrete; 50 mm bed concrete; sand bed.
- *(If on rock surface: sand bed and bed concrete not required.)*
- Stepped chutes/baffles mandatory where longitudinal bed slope exceeds critical slope.
- No-fines concrete dykes or benches in drain bottom for velocity control in steep slopes.
- Culverts in hilly terrain: spacing determined by rainfall intensity, terrain width, and ditch slope — typically **close intervals** (indicative design chart, Fig. 8.17 of standard).

### 7.4 Six-Step Hydraulic Design Procedure (Cl. 7.2.4)
1. Establish roadside plan (site data, layout, basin divides, outlets)
2. Establish cross-section data (width, depth, side slopes, conveyance check)
3. Determine channel grade (minimum 0.3%; check erosion per Table 7.1)
4. Check flow capacity using Manning's equation; adjust if inadequate
5. Determine lining/protection: if velocity > permissible → choose appropriate lining (Table 7.1)
6. Analyze outlet points and downstream effects; mitigate adverse impacts

## PART 8: CULVERT DESIGN REQUIREMENTS (Cl. 9)

### 8.1 Minimum Culvert Sizes
- Upgrading projects: hume pipe culverts **< 900 mm dia shall be replaced with minimum 1200 mm dia** (IRC:SP:13 recommendation).
- Hilly terrain minimum: **1200 mm dia** (to resist blockage by cobbles/boulders; prefer slab/box type).
- Box culverts preferred for high embankments and where sediment/debris expected.

### 8.2 Culvert Location Requirements (Cl. 9.2)
- Placed at natural depressions/valley points (identified from contour study + site visit).
- **Mandatory:** ensure runoff path to outfall from culvert outlet is available.
- Relief culverts on either side of bridges in wide flood plains.
- Minimum span of culverts crossing supercritical streams = equal to or slightly more than linear waterway at design HFL.

### 8.3 Data Required for Culvert Design (Cl. 9.4)
Mandatory data to be collected:
- Topographic maps with contours, terrain slope, soil
- Existing stream L-sections and cross-sections, HFL (from flood marks and local inquiry)
- Gauge-discharge data from CWC/state stations if available
- Hydro-meteorological data (rainfall intensity, frequency, duration)
- Inventory of existing culverts for upgrading projects (size, shape, invert levels, siltation, ponding)
- Fish passage requirements, debris size
