## CHAPTER 1 — ALIGNMENT AND FORMATION WORKS

### 1.1 Formation — Mandatory Requirements

**1.1.1** Alignment shall be finalized before formation work begins. Refer to **IRC:52** for geometric design and alignment survey.

**1.1.2** Formation components:
- Jungle clearance (understory + trees >30 cm girth)
- Earthwork in excavation: ordinary soil, soft rock, hard rock
- Earthwork in embankment with slope protection
- Temporary expedients (drains, stone soling, shingling) for traffic during construction

---

### 1.2 Soil Classification for Excavation

| Category | Description | Permissible Side Slope (H:V) |
|---|---|---|
| Ordinary/Heavy Soil | Organic soil, clay, sand, moorum, stiff clay; excavatable by picks/shovels | 1:1 to 1/2:1 |
| Ordinary/Soft Rock | Limestone, sandstone, laterite, conglomerate, weathered rock | 1/4:1 to 1/8:1 |
| Disintegrated/Loose Boulders | Excavated without blasting | Design per slope stability parameters |
| Hard Rock | Intensive drilling and blasting required | Vertical to 80°–90° from horizontal |

**Note:** Classification also based on excavation tools: spade-work, pick work, jumper work, or blasting.

---

### 1.3 Embankment/Cut & Fill — Mandatory Requirements

**1.6.1** Not more than **20% of formation width** shall be done by fill on valley side using conventional compaction methods.  
**1.6.2** No slope on cut or fill side shall be left unprotected. Protection required by gabions/wired masonry crates/breast walls/retaining walls/geo-synthetics/rock fall protection.  
**1.7.1** Profile finishing achieved by grader after main excavation; unevenness in rock cuts filled with broken pieces of appropriate size.

---

### 1.4 Special Treatments

**Table 1.1 — Applications and Functions of Geo-synthetics**

| Application Area | Geosynthetics | Functions |
|---|---|---|
| Embankments on soft soils | GT, GG | R, S |
| Retaining Walls | GG, GT | R |
| Drainage and Filtration | GT | F, S |
| Drainage-prefab composite | GC, GN | D, F, S, B |
| Erosion Control rip rap | GT | F, S |
| Sediment control-silt fence | GT | B, R, S |
| Asphalt overlay | GT, GC | B, R, S |

*GT = geotextile, GG = geogrid, GC = geocomposite, GN = geonet; R = reinforcement, S = separation, F = filtration, D = drainage, B = barrier*  
Specifications per Section 700, 3100 of MoRTH.

**Catch Water Drains (1.9.2):** Provided on hillside to intercept flow before it reaches side drain; trapezoidal shape, stone lined and cement pointed.  

**Box Cut (1.9.3):** At steep narrow spurs; requires breast walls, catch water drains, lined box drains on upper arm, breast/retaining walls on lower arm, and culverts with lined chutes.

**Zigs (1.9.5):** At fairly flat hill side; upper arm requires box cut; lower arm in cut or cut-and-fill. Extensive protective works mandatory: breast walls, catch water drains, retaining walls, culverts with chutes.

**Vision Berm/Bench Cut (1.9.6):** On sharp curves; 1.5 m bench cut on hill side inner edge; Vision Berm: 1.5 m height, 1–1.2 m wide.

**Wire Crate Gabions (1.8.3):** Size 3 m × 1.5 m × 1.5 m, per IRC specifications, IS 280 & IS 4826.

**Ledge (1.8.4):** 2 m ledge above road level to ease slope and intercept drainage.

---

### 1.5 Ecological Requirements (1.10)

- Road cutting in hills shall minimize jungle clearance (barest minimum only)
- Slope protection structures mandatory on all cut/fill slopes (tip/crown to toe)
- Muck disposed at designated locations with retaining structures and drainage
- Muck disposal sites covered with locally available vegetation
- Wildlife crossing points: increased road width at those pockets; wildlife alert signage with flashing lights; underpass/overpass with fencing

## CHAPTER 2 — DRAINAGE AND CROSS-DRAINAGE

### 2.1 Mandatory Drainage Principle

A cardinal rule: **least interference with natural drainage**. All slopes shall have stable earth surface with vegetative cover. Network of drains essential to confine and control flow and arrest adverse effects on road structures.

---

### 2.2 Hydrological Design Formulae

**Empirical Formulae for Peak Runoff:**

| Formula | Expression | Applicability |
|---|---|---|
| Dicken's Formula | Q = CM³/⁴ | General |
| Ryve's Formula | Q = CM²/³ | Tamil Nadu Hills |

Where Q = peak runoff (cumec), M = catchment area (km²), C = constant depending on rainfall.

**Manning's Formula (Velocity):**
$$V = \frac{1}{N}(R)^{2/3}(S)^{1/2}$$

Where V = mean velocity (m/s), R = A/P (hydraulic mean depth), A = cross-sectional area (m²), P = wetted perimeter (m), S = bed slope, N = coefficient of rugosity.

**Discharge by Area-Velocity Method:**
$$Q = \sum(A \times V)$$

Where strips of cross-section each have area A(1), A(2)…A(n) and velocities V(1), V(2)…V(n).

---

### 2.3 Roadside Drains — Design Standards

**2.4.1** Roadside drains shall be taken at least **300 mm below road surface** (kutcha roads).

**2.4.2(b)** Standard drain size: **60 cm × 60 cm**; gradient: **1:20 to 1:25** to develop self-cleansing velocity.

**Drain Types and Dimensions:**

| Drain Type | Width | Depth | Lining |
|---|---|---|---|
| V-Shaped | 60 cm | 45–60 cm | Stone pitching 15 cm |
| Parabolic/Saucer | 45–60 cm | 22–30 cm | Stone pitching 15 cm |
| Trapezoidal | 60 cm top, 30 cm bottom | 40–60 cm | Stone pitching or R.R. masonry |
| Toe Wall & Drain | 45 cm | 25–35 cm | Stone pitching 15 cm; toe wall 100 cm × 60 cm |
| U-Shaped | 60 cm | 60 cm | Stone pitching or R.R. masonry 15/20 cm |
| Triangular | 45–60 cm | 30–45 cm | Stone pitching |
| Kerb & Channel | 60 cm | 25–35 cm | R.R. masonry or R.S. precast CC 1:4:8 |

**2.4.2(a)** In U-shaped drains, edge stones (guide stones, whitewashed) shall be provided on roadside edge.  
**2.4.2(b)** On long stretches with steep grades, roadside drains shall be **stepped** to break velocity.  
**2.4.2(c)** Culverts: **8 to 10 culverts per km** or as per site requirement to discharge runoff to valley side.

---

### 2.4 Catch Water Drains / Intercepting Drains

**2.5.1** Trapezoidal shape, stone lined and cement pointed; must carry water to nearest cross-drainage point; cleaned and repaired before every monsoon.  
**2.5.2** Provided outside periphery of slide/unstable areas. In slide areas, additional intermediate drains may be needed.

---

### 2.5 Chutes — Mandatory Requirement

**2.6.1** Lined chutes **shall be provided mandatorily** to lead discharge to catch pit of culvert or to natural drainage channel. Box-type stepped chutes of stone masonry preferred.

**Chute Cross Section (Fig. 2.6):**
- Step size: 60 cm × 60 cm
- Construction: R.R.M. in CM 1:4, capped with P.C.C. 1:3:6
- Weep holes provided in cross walls

---

### 2.6 Sub-Surface Drainage

**2.8.1** Sub-grade moisture content shall be kept minimum. Sub-soil drains required wherever groundwater intercepts pavement.  
**2.8.2.1 Transverse Trench Drains:** Used where sub-grade water table is high; spaced at 10–20 m intervals.

**Squamish Culvert Dimensions:** Trenches ~1 m² cross-section, spaced 10–20 m; backfilled with geotextile-wrapped gravel; permeability not less than 5 × 10⁻⁴ m/s.

---

### 2.7 Cross-Drainage — Structure Selection

**Table 2.1 — Guidelines for Choice of Cross Drainage Structures**

| Structure | Location/Suitability |
|---|---|
| Ford | Non-perennial stream; max depth ≤ 1.6 m; interruption ≤ 12 hrs/stretch for ODR, ≤ 24 hrs for VR; ≤ 10 days/year ODR, ≤ 15 days/year VR; very low traffic |
| Paved Dip/Low-level Causeway | Same as Ford; somewhat higher traffic |
| Vented Causeway | Shallow streams with permanent dry weather flow >0.6 m; same interruption criteria as Ford |
| Submersible Bridge | Where high-level bridge is too expensive |
| Culvert | High level; span between abutments ≤ 6 m; narrow streams |
| High Level Bridge | Maximum possible flood discharge; deep narrow streams/ravines |
| Steel Equipment Bridges (Portable) | Emergency need |
| Suspension Bridges | Long and deep gaps in hilly terrain |
| Boat Ferry/Pontoon | Large streams where bridge not economical |

---

### 2.8 Culverts — Design Requirements

**2.10.5.1** Culvert = bridge with length ≤ 6 m between abutment faces.  
**Rate:** 8–10 culverts per km of hill road.  
**Minimum vent:** 1.0 m × 1.5 m or as per design.

**Pipe Culvert (2.10.5.2a):**
- Diameter: ≥ 90 cm, preferably 100–120 cm
- Pipe gradient: not flatter than 1:30
- Cushion required: 60 cm or more; suitable only where cushion is available
- Concrete bedding mandatory below pipes

**Arch Culvert (2.10.5.2b):** Abutments, wing walls, arch, parapets and foundations in stone masonry or concrete.

**Slab Culvert (2.10.5.2c):** RCC slab with or without beams; deck slab designed as one-way slab.

**Box/Precast Segmental Box Culvert (2.10.5.2d):** RCC rectangular cross-section; base slab, abutment walls and slabs connected monolithically; single or multiple box cells.

**Protective Works (Mandatory):** Catch pit, stepped guide walls, chutes, and apron at discharge point to dissipate kinetic energy.

## CHAPTER 3 — SLOPE STABILITY, LANDSLIDE & ROCK SLOPE INVESTIGATION

### 3.1 Classification of Slope Movements (3.1.1)

- Falls (Rock/debris/earth falls)
- Topples
- Slides (Rotational and Translational)
- Spreads
- Flows (Debris flow, Earth flow)
- Complex (Combination)

### 3.2 Causes of Slope Movements (Mandatory to Document in DPR)

- Geological factors (lithology, structure, discontinuities)
- Change in slope gradient (undercutting by streams, road cuts)
- Surcharge (structures, fills)
- Shocks and vibrations (blasting, earthquakes)
- Weathering
- Development activities
- Water/Pore water pressure

### 3.3 Landslide Investigation Requirements

**Field Investigations Mandatory:**
- Topographical mapping of area
- Geological and geomorphological mapping
- Hydrological investigations
- Geophysical investigations
- Geotechnical investigations (bore holes, test pits, SPT, etc.)

**Laboratory Investigations Required:**
- Classification and index property tests
- Strength and deformation tests
- Permeability tests
- Rock strength properties tests
- Laboratory tests on rocks

### 3.4 Proforma Requirements (3.4)

DPR shall include completed proforma for:
- Slope instability/landslide occurrence data
- Slope clearance history

### 3.5 Stability Analysis Methods

**Soil Slopes (3.5.2.1):** Limit Equilibrium Methods  
**Rock Slopes (3.5.2.3):** Rock mass classification (RMR, SMR), kinematic analysis, numerical methods

**Minimum Factor of Safety:**
- Static condition: 1.5
- Pseudo-static (seismic): 1.2

---

## CHAPTER 4 — SLOPE STABILITY, LANDSLIDE & ROCKFALL MITIGATION

### 4.1 Protection, Prevention & Stabilization Hierarchy

1. **Avoid the problem** — re-alignment to bypass unstable areas
2. **Reduce driving forces** — drainage, excavation, unloading
3. **Increase resisting forces** — retaining structures, anchors, vegetation

---

### 4.2 Retaining Walls — Classification and Requirements (4.3.3.2)

**Types of Retaining Walls:**

| Type | Characteristics |
|---|---|
| Gravity Wall (Stone/PCC/CC) | Self-weight for stability; no reinforcement |
| Cantilever Wall | RCC; T or L shaped; economical 3–7 m |
| Counterfort Wall | RCC with counterforts at 1.5–3 m spacing; >7 m height |
| Buttress Wall | Counterforts on fill side |
| Breast Wall | Low retaining wall on cut slope face |
| Crib Wall | Interlocking precast concrete/timber members filled with granular material |
| Gabion Wall | Wire mesh baskets filled with stones |
| Reinforced Earth | Geosynthetic or metallic strips with fill |

**Minimum Design Requirements:**
- Sliding: Factor of Safety ≥ 1.5
- Overturning: FOS ≥ 2.0
- Bearing Capacity: FOS ≥ 3.0
- Weep holes mandatory: 75 mm dia at 1.5 m spacing (horizontal and vertical)
- Filter material behind wall mandatory

**Gabion Specifications (Wire Crate):**
- Standard size: 3 m × 1.5 m × 1.5 m
- Wire: IS 280 & IS 4826
- Slope: tilted towards hillside (batter angle)
- Used as breast walls up to 3 m height; stepped above 3 m

---

### 4.3 Rockfall Protection (4.3.4)

**Passive Systems:**
- Rock catch ditches
- Catch fences (wire rope/chain link); rated for impact energy
- Catch nets
- Rock sheds (reinforced concrete or steel with earth cushion)

**Active Systems:**
- Rock bolts and anchors
- Wire mesh draping
- Shotcrete
- Rock scaling (removing loose blocks)

**Mandatory for Rock Cutting:** Post-blasting inspection and removal of dangerous overhangs before opening to traffic.

---

### 4.4 Debris Flow Protection (4.3.4.2)

- Open/closed check dams to retard flow and trap debris
- Sabo dams for major debris flows
- Deflection berms on valley side
- Emergency measures: temporary closure, warning signs, diversion

---

### 4.5 Instrumentation & Monitoring Requirements (4.4)

**Mandatory Monitoring Instruments:**
- Piezometers (for pore water pressure monitoring)
- Inclinometers (sub-surface movement monitoring)
- Surface movement markers (pegs/pins at 5–10 m grid)

**Techniques for Natural Hazard Monitoring (4.4.6):**
- Rain gauges
- Slope movement sensors
- Early warning systems with threshold values

## CHAPTER 5 — PAVEMENT DESIGN

### 5.1 General Design Principles for Hill Roads

Special considerations (not needed in plains):
- Sub-grade = mixture of gravel, boulders, rock materials with fine-grained matrix
- High annual rainfall (up to 1200–1500 mm in 6 months); prolonged saturated road bed
- Thermal stresses due to large temperature variation
- High-altitude UV exposure hardens and embrittles bitumen
- Snow, frost heave, icing in high-altitude areas
- Limited construction season at high altitude

**5.1.2g (Mandatory):** Cut formation serving as sub-grade shall be prepared to achieve desired CBR and to receive sub-base/base course as directed by Engineer.

---

### 5.2 Subgrade Requirements

**Table 5.1 — Subgrade Material Categories (Indicative CBR)**

| Category | Subgrade Type | CBR Range |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Rocky subgrade, conglomerates, slate | High strength — CBR not criterion |
| 2 | Gravel with binder soil <20% hard slate | Soaked CBR 8–15 |
| 3 | Gravel with binder soil >20% shale of medium hardness, silty sands | Soaked CBR 5–10 |
| 4 | Silty clays and soft shale | Soaked CBR 2–5 |

**Mandatory CBR Requirements:**
- Minimum effective CBR for design per IRC:37: **5%**
- Expansive clays/silty expansive clays (high swelling per IS 1498, Table 8): soak for **7 days** (not 4 days) before determining CBR
- Subgrade CBR test: sieve through 20 mm, compact at Modified Proctor density
- Sub-grade CBR <2%: provide capping layer 150 mm thick with minimum CBR 10%
- Fine sand or clay with PI >15 and/or clay content >50%: 100 mm insulating layer of screening/coarse sand on subgrade
- Expansive (black cotton) soil: non-expansive buffer layer OR 225 mm blanket course of coarse/medium sand or non-plastic moorum (PI <5)
- Salt infestation areas (salt concentration >0.2%): capillary cut-off layer of coarse sand 225 mm on sub-grade
- Dense/low density material: loosen to 500 mm depth, recompact in layers per IS specifications

---

### 5.3 Traffic Design Parameters

**5.4 Traffic Growth Rate:**
- Initial traffic: 7-day, 24-hour peak season classified traffic count
- Minimum growth rate: **5%** (if actual data unavailable or <5%)
- Traffic census: IRC:9; growth forecast: IRC:108

**5.5 Design Traffic:** Cumulative number of standard axles as per IRC:37.

**5.6 Design Period:**
- NH, SH, Urban roads: minimum **20 years** (excluding construction period)
- Other categories: minimum **15 years**
- Initial construction stage (stage construction): not less than **10 years**

### 5.4 Pavement Layer Standards

**5.2.1 Rigid Pavement:** Not generally recommended for hill roads (seismic damage risk). Rigid pavement permitted at: bus depots, toll plazas, passing lanes, in front of habitations, waterfalls, hairpin bends, over culverts, causeways, bridges.

**5.2.2 Flexible Pavement Layers:**
- **(a) Sub-Grade:** Top 50 cm of road bed; compacted to achieve desired CBR
- **(b) Sub-Base:** Granular (natural sand, moorum, gravel, laterite, kanker, brick metal, crushed stone, crushed slag); per MoRTH Table 401; thickness per IRC:37
- **(c) Base Course:** WMM or equivalent granular material per MoRTH; per IRC:37
- **(d) Wearing/Binder Course:** Surface Dressing, Mix-Seal, Surfacing, Open Graded Premix, SDBC, or BC; Binder = BM or DBM; per MoRTH Specifications and IRC:37

---

### 5.5 Special Pavement for Snow/High Altitude Areas

**5.10.1 Heavy Rainfall Areas (>300 cm annual rainfall):**
- Effective drainage network mandatory
- Formation width on hill side surfaced up to line drains (no gap between pavement edge and drain)
- Dense bituminous mixes: BC at surface, DBM at base/binder course
- Mastic asphalt as alternative surface course

**5.11.1 Snow Fall Areas — Altitude Classification:**
- 2500–3000 m altitude: continuous winter snow clearance possible; normal flexible pavement with specific provisions
- Above 3000 m (Great Himalayan ranges, snow bound in winter): roads closed in winter; special high-altitude pavement design required

**5.11.3 High Altitude Pavement (above 3000 m, snow bound in winter) — Special Requirements:**
- Bitumen grade: **VG-10 or VG-20** for BC/DBM
- Base: W.M.M; Sub-base: G.S.B. per MoRTH
- Minimum total pavement depth: **45 cm** (non-frost susceptible material)
- Structurally strong course (BM/DBM) preferred over granular bases (WBM/WMM) where heavy snow clearance machinery uses the road
- Crushed stone base preferred (non-frost susceptible, free draining, may extend over full formation width)

**5.11.6 Pavement Composition for Snow Bound Areas — Frost Penetration Formula:**
$$Z = A\frac{\sqrt{48KF}}{L}$$

Where Z = depth of frost penetration, K = thermal conductivity, F = air freezing index (degree-days), L = volumetric heat of latent fusion, A = dimensionless coefficient (fusion parameter and thermal radiation).

### 5.10 Frost Susceptibility Classification (Table 5.2)

| Group | Designation | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| F₁ | Gravel soils with 3–20% finer than 0.02 mm | Least frost susceptible; least thaw weakening |
| F₂ | Sands with 3–15% finer than 0.02 mm | Increased frost susceptibility and thaw weakening |
| F₃ | Gravel >20% finer than 0.02 mm; sands >15% finer than 0.02 mm; clays PI >12; varved clays (uniform subgrade) | Frost susceptible; high thaw weakening |
| F₄ | All silts including silty clays; fine silty sands >15% finer than 0.02 mm; lean clays PI <12; varved clays (non-uniform) | Frost susceptible; high thaw weakening |

*Well-graded soils with ≥3% particles <0.02 mm = highly frost susceptible. Uniformly graded soils with ≥10% particles <0.02 mm = frost susceptible.*

## CHAPTER 6 — SNOW CLEARANCE AND AVALANCHE TREATMENT

### 6.1 Snow Categories by Altitude

| Altitude | Condition |
|---|---|
| <2500 m | Normal rainfall areas; standard pavement |
| 2500–3000 m | Snowfall areas; continuous clearance possible |
| >3000 m (Great Himalayas) | Snow-bound in winter; roads closed; summer clearance operation |

### 6.2 Avalanche Control Structures (6.5)

**Formation Zone Structures (6.5.4):**
- Supporting structures (snow rakes, snow bridges, snow nets) to prevent snow from releasing

**Middle/Transit Zone Structures (6.5.5):**
- Deflecting structures, guiding walls, braking mounds

**Run-off Zone Structures (6.5.6):**
- Catching/retaining structures (catching dams, walls, nets)

**Avalanche Pressure Formula:**
$$P = \rho v^2 \sin^2\theta + \rho_s g h \cos\theta$$

Where P = avalanche pressure, ρ = density of snow, v = velocity, θ = slope angle, h = snow depth, g = gravitational acceleration.

### 6.3 Snow Clearance Operations (6.6)

| Method | Application |
|---|---|
| Anti-icing | Surface treatment before snow event; Salt/Brine spray; Sand/Abrasive spray; CMA; Potassium Acetate |
| De-icing | Surface treatment after snow event |
| Mechanical Removal | Crawler tractor (blade plough), Rotary snow plough/cutter, Wheeled dozers (blade plough), Drag scrapers |

**Advance Planning (6.8):**
- Snow markers: provided in advance of snow season to mark road edges
- Equipment: as per requirement and road classification
- Manpower: deployed in advance
