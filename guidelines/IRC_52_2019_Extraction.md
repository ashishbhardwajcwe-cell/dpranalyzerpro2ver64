# IRC:52-2019 — Alignment Survey & Geometric Design of Hill Roads
## Structured Extraction for DPR Analyzer Pro
**Document:** IRC:52-2019 (Third Revision, August 2019)
**Applicability:** New hill roads and improvement of existing hill roads (excluding urban roads/city streets in hilly terrain)
**Terrain Coverage:** Mountainous Terrain (cross slope 25–60%) and Steep Terrain (cross slope >60%)

---

## PART 1: TERRAIN CLASSIFICATION

| Terrain Type | Cross Slope |
|---|---|
| Plain | < 10% |
| Rolling | 10% to 25% |
| Mountainous | > 25% to 60% |
| Steep | > 60% |

> **DPR Check:** Verify terrain classification used in DPR matches actual cross-slope measurements at site.

---

## PART 2: KEY DEFINITIONS (Compliance-Critical)

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Ruling Gradient | Maximum gradient not to be exceeded in normal course |
| Limiting Gradient | Steeper than ruling; used in restricted lengths where ruling is not feasible |
| Exceptional Gradient | Steeper than limiting; used in short stretches (≤100 m) in extraordinary situations |
| Escape Gradient | Reverse grade with crash barrier adjoining exceptional gradient for brake-failure scenarios |
| Climbing Lane | Separate additional lane for uphill traffic on continuous exceptional gradient |
| Hair-pin Bend | Bend causing reversal of traffic direction |
| Roadway Width | Carriageway + shoulder widths (excludes parapets and side drains) |
| Sight Distance | Distance along road surface at which driver has visibility at specified height |
| Superelevation | Inward transverse tilt at horizontal curves to reduce centrifugal force effect |
| Passing Place | Area provided on road side for vehicles to cross/give way |
| Lateral Clearance | Distance from extreme carriageway edge to face of nearest structure/obstruction |
| Vertical Clearance | Height from highest carriageway point to lowest overhead structure/rock face |

## PART 7: CLEARANCES

### 7.1 Lateral Clearance (Clause 6.7.1)
- Minimum = normal shoulder width
- Desirably full roadway width at approaches shall be carried through underpasses

### 7.2 Vertical Clearance (Clause 6.7.2)
- **Minimum: 5.50 m** over entire roadway at all underpasses, overhanging cliffs, and semi-tunnel sections
- Measured from highest carriageway point (crown or superelevated edge) to lowest overhead structure/rock outcrop
- Due allowance for future pavement strengthening/raising required

## PART 8: HORIZONTAL ALIGNMENT

### 8.1 General Rules (Clause 6.8.1)
- Alignment must be fluent, blending with natural contours
- **Avoid:** sharp curves at end of long tangents, breaks at drainage structures
- Minimum curve length: **150 m for 5° deflection angle**; increase by 30 m for each 1° decrease in deflection angle
- Deflection < 1°: no curve design required
- Broken-back curves: avoid; if unavoidable, minimum tangent between = 10 sec travel time
- Compound curves: radius ratio of flatter to sharper ≤ **1.5:1**
- Reverse curves: transition curves must be provided; no overlap of tangent points

### 8.2 Superelevation (Clause 6.8.2)

**Formula:**
```
e = V² / (225 × R)
where:
  e = superelevation (m/m)
  V = speed (km/h)
  R = radius (m)
```

**Maximum Superelevation:**
| Condition | Maximum e |
|---|---|
| Snow-bound areas | 7% |
| Hilly areas not bound by snow | 10% |
| Hair-pin bends | 10% |

**Rate of Change of Superelevation (Clause 6.8.2.10):**
| Terrain | Maximum Rate |
|---|---|
| Plain and Rolling | 1 in 150 |
| Mountainous and Steep | 1 in 60 |

### 8.5 Transition (Spiral) Curve Length (Clause 6.8.4)

**Formula 1 — Rate of Change of Centrifugal Acceleration:**
```
Ls = 0.0215 × V³ / (C × R)
where C = 80/(75+V), max 0.8, min 0.5
```

**Formula 2 — Rate of Change of Superelevation:**
```
For Plain/Rolling:   Ls = 2.7 × V² / R
For Mountainous/Steep: Ls = 1.0 × V² / R
```
> Use the larger value from both formulas.

### 8.8 Set-Back Distance — Table 6.11 (Single-Lane)

| Curve Radius (m) | S=20m (V=20) | S=25m (V=25) | S=30m (V=30) | S=45m (V=40) | S=60m (V=50) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 14 | 3.4 | — | — | — | — |
| 15 | 3.2 | — | — | — | — |
| 20 | 2.4 | 3.8 | — | — | — |
| 30 | 1.7 | 2.6 | 3.7 | — | — |
| 50 | 1.0 | 1.6 | 2.2 | 5.0 | — |
| 80 | — | 1.0 | 1.4 | 3.1 | 5.6 |
| 100 | — | 0.8 | 1.1 | 2.5 | 4.5 |
| 150 | — | 0.5 | 0.8 | 1.7 | 2.3 |

**Formula:**
```
m = R − (R − n) × cos(θ)
where θ = S / [2(R − n)] radians
  m = set-back distance from road centreline (m)
  R = centreline radius (m)
  n = distance between road centreline and inside lane centreline (m)
  S = sight distance (m)
(For single-lane roads: n = 0, sight distance along centreline)
```


## PART 9: VERTICAL ALIGNMENT

### 9.1 Recommended Gradients — Table 6.12

| Gradient Classification | Mountainous & Steep Terrain >3000 m MSL | Steep Terrain ≤3000 m MSL |
|---|---|---|
| Ruling | 5% (1 in 20.0) | 6% (1 in 16.7) |
| Limiting | 6% (1 in 16.7) | 7% (1 in 14.3) |
| Exceptional | 7% (1 in 14.3) | 8% (1 in 12.5) |

**Rules:**
- Exceptional gradient: maximum **100 m at a stretch**; successive stretches separated by minimum **100 m** of gentler gradient
- Cumulative rise/fall over 2 km: max **100 m** in mountainous terrain; max **120 m** in steep terrain
- Grade compensation at curves not required when gradient < 4%

### 9.2 Grade Compensation at Curves (Clause 6.9.2)
```
Grade Compensation (%) = (30 + R) / R
subject to maximum of 75/R (where R = radius in meters)
```
- Not applicable when gradient ≤ 4%

### 9.3 Escape Lanes (Clause 6.9.1.8)
- Required where continuous descending grade ≥ 2 km
- Emergency escape ramp at approximately every 2 km interval
- Ascending type with arrester bed (preferred)
- Design speed range: 50–120 km/h
- Arrester bed width: 9–12 m recommended (range 3.6–12 m in use)
- Minimum separation from carriageway: 1.0 m
- Service road alongside arrester bed: ≥ 3 m width (desirable)

### 9.4 Climbing Lanes (Clause 6.9.1.9)
- Required on continuous exceptional gradients
- Enables safe overtaking for uphill vehicles
- Clear signing, road marking, and physical barriers required

### 9.5 Vertical Curves (Clause 6.9.3)
- All grade changes exceeding Table 6.13 values require vertical curves
- Type: parabolic (square parabola)
- Broken-back grade lines (two vertical curves same direction separated by short tangent): **avoid**

### 9.6 Minimum Length of Vertical Curves — Table 6.13

| Design Speed (km/h) | Max Grade Change NOT Requiring Curve (%) | Min Length of Vertical Curve (m) |
|---|---|---|
| Up to 35 | 1.5 | 15 |
| 40 | 1.2 | 20 |
| 50 | 1.0 | 30 |

### 9.7 Summit Curve Lengths (Clause 6.9.4)

**For Stopping Sight Distance:**
```
When L > S:  L = N × S² / 4.4
When L < S:  L = 2S − (4.4 / N)
```

**For Intermediate Sight Distance:**
```
When L > S:  L = N × S² / 9.6
When L < S:  L = 2S − (9.6 / N)
```
*(N = algebraic difference of grades; L = curve length m; S = sight distance m)*

### 9.8 Valley Curve Lengths (Clause 6.9.5)

**Based on headlight beam distance = stopping sight distance:**
```
When L > S:  L = N × S² / (1.50 + 0.035 × S)
When L < S:  L = 2S − [(1.50 + 0.35S) / N]
```

## PART 16: DPR COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST — IRC:52-2019

### A. GENERAL & CLASSIFICATION
- [ ] Terrain correctly classified as Mountainous or Steep (cross-slope % documented)
- [ ] Road category (NH/SH/MDR/ODR/VR) established and matches design standards used
- [ ] IRC:52-2019 cited as governing standard for geometric design
- [ ] IRC:SP:48 referenced for Hill Road Manual details
- [ ] Environmental impact assessment reference to IRC:SP:19-2001 included

### B. ROAD LAND & CROSS-SECTION
- [ ] Road land (ROW) width complies with Table 6.1 for terrain type and road class
- [ ] Building line setback ≥ 5 m from road land boundary confirmed
- [ ] Carriageway width correct per Table 6.2 for road class and terrain
- [ ] Hill-side and valley-side shoulder widths separately specified
- [ ] Additional ROW for deep cuts/fills/unstable areas accounted for
- [ ] Strategic/border roads designed for ≥ two-lane + full shoulder widths
- [ ] Bridge clear width ≥ 7.5 m for double-lane bridges
- [ ] Snow clearance areas: roadway width increased by 1.5 m if required
- [ ] Hard rock/unstable sections: width reduction ≤ 0.8 m; passing places provided

### C. DESIGN SPEED
- [ ] Ruling design speed selected from Table 6.4 for road class and terrain
- [ ] Minimum design speed used only where ruling speed is not feasible (documented reason)
- [ ] Design speed consistent throughout connected stretches

### D. CAMBER / CROSS FALL
- [ ] Camber value selected per surface type from Clause 6.4.2
- [ ] High rainfall areas use steeper end of camber range
- [ ] Earth shoulder cross fall ≥ pavement camber + 0.5% (minimum 3%)
- [ ] Paved shoulder cross fall matches surface type
- [ ] Superelevated sections: outer earthen shoulder reverse cross fall = 0.5% provided

### E. SIGHT DISTANCE
- [ ] Stopping sight distance (SSD) complies with Table 6.5 for design speed
- [ ] Intermediate sight distance (ISD) provided wherever possible
- [ ] Measurement criteria per Table 6.6 (eye height 1.2 m, object height 0.15 m for SSD)
- [ ] Vision berms/benching provided at curves as required
- [ ] Set-back distances cleared per Table 6.11 (single-lane) or formula (two-lane)
- [ ] Blind curves minimized; where unavoidable, countermeasures (benching/width/one-way) documented

### F. CLEARANCES
- [ ] Lateral clearance ≥ shoulder width at underpasses
- [ ] Vertical clearance ≥ **5.50 m** at all underpasses, overhanging cliffs, semi-tunnels
- [ ] Allowance for future pavement strengthening included in vertical clearance

### G. HORIZONTAL ALIGNMENT
- [ ] Minimum curve radius not less than ruling minimum per Table 6.8
- [ ] Where absolute minimum radius used: documented exceptional circumstances
- [ ] Superelevation ≤ 10% (non-snow) or ≤ 7% (snow-bound)
- [ ] Superelevation formula verified: e = V²/(225R)
- [ ] Rate of change of superelevation ≤ 1:60 (mountainous/steep)
- [ ] Superelevation radii waiver checked against Table 6.7
- [ ] Transition curve length ≥ values from Table 6.9 (or calculated formula, whichever greater)
- [ ] Carriageway widening at curves per Table 6.10 provided
- [ ] Compound curves: radius ratio ≤ 1.5:1
- [ ] Broken-back curves avoided or minimum 10-sec tangent provided
- [ ] Curve length ≥ 150 m for 5° deflection; adjusted for smaller deflection angles
- [ ] Hair-pin bends minimized; located on stable slopes (not in valleys)
- [ ] No series of hair-pin bends on same hill face

### H. HAIR-PIN BENDS (if applicable)
- [ ] Minimum inner radius ≥ 14 m
- [ ] Minimum transition length ≥ 15 m
- [ ] Gradient at bend ≤ 2.5% and ≥ 0.5%
- [ ] Superelevation at bend = 10%
- [ ] Minimum roadway width at apex per road class
- [ ] Successive bends separated by ≥ 60 m
- [ ] Cross drainage 20 m before bend provided
- [ ] Full roadway width surfaced at bend

### I. VERTICAL ALIGNMENT & GRADIENTS
- [ ] Ruling gradient not exceeded without documented justification
- [ ] Limiting gradient used only where topography compels; length minimized
- [ ] Exceptional gradient: ≤ 100 m stretch; successive stretches separated by ≥ 100 m
- [ ] Cumulative rise/fall over 2 km ≤ 100 m (mountainous) or ≤ 120 m (steep)
- [ ] Grade compensation at horizontal curves calculated and applied
- [ ] Vertical curves provided for all grade changes exceeding Table 6.13 thresholds
- [ ] Summit curve lengths comply with formulae (SSD and ISD checks)
- [ ] Valley curve lengths comply with headlight beam formula
- [ ] Broken-back grade lines avoided
- [ ] Decks of culverts/minor bridges follow same grade as road

### J. ESCAPE LANES & CLIMBING LANES
- [ ] Escape lane required where descending grade ≥ 2 km (check and confirm)
- [ ] Escape ramp interval ≈ 2 km on qualifying grades
- [ ] Ascending-type arrester bed escape ramp designed
- [ ] Escape ramp design speed: 50–120 km/h
- [ ] Climbing lane provided on continuous exceptional gradients

### K. PASSING PLACES (Single-Lane Roads)
- [ ] Passing places at 2–3 per km on single-lane roads
- [ ] Size: 3.75 m wide × 30 m (inside) × 20 m (farther side)
- [ ] Location judiciously selected for visibility and extra width

### L. CAPACITY
- [ ] Design service volume compared with Table 6.3 values
- [ ] Road classification appropriate for projected traffic volumes
- [ ] If paved shoulders ≥ 1.5 m: 15% capacity increase noted

### M. CO-ORDINATION OF ALIGNMENTS
- [ ] Horizontal and vertical alignments designed in unison (3D effect)
- [ ] Vertical curves coincide with horizontal curves as far as possible
- [ ] Sharp horizontal curves avoided at/near summit/sag curve apexes

### N. TUNNELS (if applicable)
- [ ] Horizontal control accuracy 1:30,000 established
- [ ] Tunnel alignment as straight as possible; curve radius ≥ 200 m (min 100 m)
- [ ] Gradient 0.2% minimum; ≤ 4% for tunnels >300 m
- [ ] Artificial ventilation designed for tunnels >400 m
- [ ] Geological/geophysical study by expert geologist included
- [ ] Portal location on sound rock, free from faults, not affected by landslides

### O. SURVEY ADEQUACY
- [ ] Survey done using DGPS + Total Station (accuracy ±1 mm documented)
- [ ] Benchmark interval ≤ 250 m established
- [ ] Cross-sections at ≤ 20 m intervals (straights) and ≤ 10 m (curves)
- [ ] Control pillars established per specification
- [ ] Final centre line reference pillars ≤ 100 m spacing, beyond expected cut edge
