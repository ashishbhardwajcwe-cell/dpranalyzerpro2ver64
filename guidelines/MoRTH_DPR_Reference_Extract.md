## SECTION 300 — EARTHWORK, EROSION CONTROL AND DRAINAGE

### Clause 305 — Embankment Construction

#### 305.2.1 Unsuitable Materials (PROHIBITED)
- Materials from swamps, marshes, bogs
- Peat, log, stump, perishable matter; soils classified as OL, OI, OH or Pt per IS:1498
- Materials susceptible to spontaneous combustion
- Frozen materials
- Clay with **Liquid Limit > 70** and **Plasticity Index > 45**
- Materials with leachable salts

#### 305.2.1.2 Expansive Clay
- Free Swelling Index **> 50%** (IS:2720 Part 40) → NOT permitted as fill
- Subgrade and top **500 mm** of embankment below subgrade → must be **non-expansive**

#### 305.2.1.3 Sulphate Limits
- Soluble sulphate **> 1.9 g SO₃/litre** (BS:1377 Test 10, 2:1 water-soil) → not within **500 mm** of concrete
- Total sulphate **> 0.5% by mass** (BS:1377 Test 9) → not within **500 mm** of metallic items

#### 305.2.1.4 Maximum Particle Size
- In embankment: **75 mm**
- In subgrade: **50 mm**
- Max particle size ≤ **2/3 of compacted layer thickness**

#### TABLE 300-1 — Density Requirements of Embankment and Subgrade Materials

| S.No. | Type of Work | Min. Lab Dry Unit Weight |
|-------|--------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | Embankments ≤ 3 m height, not subject to flooding | ≥ 15.2 kN/m³ |
| 2 | Embankments > 3 m height OR subject to prolonged inundation | ≥ 16.0 kN/m³ |
| 3 | Subgrade and earthen shoulders/verges/backfill | ≥ 17.5 kN/m³ |

*Test: IS:2720 (Part 8)*

#### TABLE 300-2 — Compaction Requirements for Embankment and Subgrade

| Type of Work/Material | Relative Compaction (% of max. lab dry density per IS:2720 Part 8) |
|-----------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Subgrade and earthen shoulders | ≥ 97% |
| Embankment | ≥ 95% |
| Expansive Clays — Subgrade + 500 mm below | NOT ALLOWED |
| Expansive Clays — Remaining embankment | ≥ 90% |

#### 305.3.5 Layer Construction Requirements
- Layer thickness: **≤ 200 mm compacted thickness**
- Moisture at compaction: **OMC −2% to OMC +1%** (IS:2720 Part 7 or Part 8)
- Clod size in embankment: **≤ 75 mm**; in subgrade: **≤ 50 mm**

#### 305.3.6 Compaction Equipment
- Vibratory roller: **80 to 100 kN static weight**
- OR heavy pneumatic-tyred roller of adequate capacity
- Each layer compacted to densities in **Table 300-2** before next layer
- Test: IS:2720 (Part 28)

#### 305.4.1 Widening of Existing Embankment
- Side slopes steeper than **1V:4H** → cut continuous horizontal benches **≥ 300 mm wide**
- Slope flatter than **1V:4H** → plough/scarify acceptable

#### 305.4.6 Embankment Under Water
- Compaction: mechanical tamping, **≥ 90% relative compaction** using IS:2720 (Part 8)

#### Key Requirements
- Ground shall be loosened to **0.5 m below subgrade** if density < 97% relative compaction
- Unsuitable material in foundation → remove and replace minimum **500 mm**

Natural sand, moorum, gravel, crushed stone, or combinations

- Layer ≤ 100 mm → smooth wheeled roller **80–100 kN**

- Roller speed: **≤ 5 km/h**
- Moisture: **OMC −2% to OMC +1%**

### Clause 602 — Cement Concrete Pavement

#### 602.1 Scope
- Unreinforced, dowel-jointed plain cement concrete (PCC) pavement
- Design parameters (slab thickness, concrete grade, joint details) as per drawings

#### 602.2.2 Cement
- OPC 33 Grade (IS:269), OPC 43 Grade (IS:8112), OPC 53 Grade (IS:12269) — **43 Grade preferred as minimum**
- Sulphate-resistant cement (IS:12330) if soil sulphates exceed **0.5%**
- Material approval **≥ 45 days** before commencement

#### 602.2.3 Admixtures
- Per IS:6925 and IS:9103
- Air-entrained concrete: **5 ± 1.5%** air for 25 mm nominal aggregate

#### 602.2.4 Aggregates
- Per IS:383; LA Abrasion **≤ 35%**
- Max coarse aggregate size: **25 mm**
- Water absorption of coarse aggregate: **≤ 2%**
- Soundness (5 cycles): Na₂SO₄ **≤ 12%**; MgSO₄ **≤ 18%**
- Chloride content: **≤ 0.06%** by weight
- Sulphate content (SO₃): **≤ 0.25%** by weight
- Fine aggregate: clay lumps **≤ 4%**; coal/lignite **≤ 1%**; passing 75 micron **≤ 4%**

#### 602.3.2 Cement Content
- **Minimum: 350 kg/m³**
- **Maximum: 425 kg/m³**

#### 602.3.3 Concrete Strength
- **Max free water-cement ratio: 0.50**
- Design mix based on **flexural strength**
- Field quality control: **flexural strength**
- Slump: **30 ± 15 mm** (typical range, may be modified per paving equipment)

#### 602.4 Sub-base
- DLC sub-base per Clause 601
- Cracks in sub-base wider than **10 mm** → repair before laying separation membrane

#### 602.5 Separation Membrane
- Impermeable plastic sheeting: **125 microns thick**, laid flat without creases
- Overlap: **≥ 300 mm**

#### 602.6.2.2 Contraction Joints
- Mechanically sawn joint groove: **3 to 5 mm wide**, depth **1/4 to 1/3 of slab ± 5 mm**
- Saw cutting: as soon as concrete can bear load of saw machine (typically **6–8 hours**)

#### 602.6.4 Longitudinal Joints
- Saw cut to **≥ 1/3 slab depth ± 5 mm**
- Tie bars per Clause 602.6.6

#### 602.6.5 Dowel Bars
- Mild steel rounds per IS:432/IS:1139 (Grade S240)
- Positioned at **mid-depth ± 20 mm**
- Centred on joint line **± 25 mm**
- Alignment: **± 3 mm per 300 mm** length (2/3 bars) and **± 2 mm per 300 mm** (remaining bars)
- Plastic sheath length: **≥ 2/3 of bar** (contraction joints) or **half + 50 mm** (expansion joints)
- Max bond stress through sheath (pull-out test): **≤ 0.14 MPa**

#### 602.6.6 Tie Bars
- Deformed steel, **415 MPa** per IS:1786
- Position: middle third of slab depth
- Corrosion protection: bituminous paint **75 mm** on each side of joint
- Minimum cover below joint groove: **30 mm**

#### 602.7 Weather Limits
- **No concreting when concrete temperature > 30°C** or **< 5°C (descending)**
- Max time from water addition to surface finishing: **120 min** (concrete < 25°C) / **90 min** (25–30°C)
- Trucks/tippers not to run on completed slabs until **28 days** after placing

#### 602.8 Side Forms / Rails
- Straight within **3 mm in 3 m**; settlement **≤ 1.5 mm in 3 m**
- Secured by **≥ 3 stakes/pins per 3 m length**
- Set to line **± 10 mm** and level **± 3 mm**
- Forms inspected **12 hours before** use and not removed until **12 hours after** concreting

#### 602.8.2 Form Setting in Advance
- Forms set for **≥ 200 m** length in advance of paving, or next 24 hrs work (whichever more)

#### 602.9.1 Paving Rate Capacity
- Batching plant + paving equipment capable of **≥ 300 m per day**

#### 602.9.3.3 Paver Specifications
- Slip form or fixed form paver with electronic controls
- Vibrators: frequency **8,300 to 9,600 impulses/min**; spacing **≤ 60 cm**

## KEY ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA FORMULA (903.2.2, 903.3.1, 903.4.2)

**Compaction, Marshall stability, and stabiliser content acceptance:**

> Mean value ≥ Specified value + **[1.65 − 1.65/(√n − 0.5)] × σ**

where n = number of samples, σ = standard deviation
